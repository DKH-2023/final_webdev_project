Tools and resources

Content writing is hard work!

As someone new to the world of UX/Content Design, I am intimidated. Daily.

So I have done my research. I've found good, bad and ugly resources. I thought I'd share the good.

Read this page to discover:

  • Websites

If I can help just one person on their journey, I will be happy.

Useful websites

A few online resources that you may find helpful:

Content Design London's website is an invaluable resource. It provides a wealth of information, insights, and tips to help you excel in the field of content design.

Explore their website to access a treasure trove of knowledge and expertise to fuel your content design journey.

Gov UK's content design website is an essential resource for new content designers.

It provides valuable insights, guidelines, and standards to create impactful and user-centric content, setting you on the path to success in the field.

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2

Unlock digital accessibility for all with WCAG 2.2, the latest standard from W3C – making the web a better place for everyone.

Useful YouTube channels

YouTube hosts millions of videos. This can be overwhelming.

To help, I have selected a few videos I have found useful.

Check out their YouTube channels, where you can gain valuable insights and tips from experienced content creators:

Nielsen Norman Group

Nielsen Norman Group is known for its expertise in usability and user experience.

Nielsen Norman Group's channel offers insights into content design, web usability, and user research.

Content Design London

Content Design London is a leading authority in content strategy, offering invaluable resources and expertise for content creators.

Content Design London's channel hosts videos covering a wide range of topics to help you excel in the field.

Silktide

Silktide's mission is to help people understand and improve their websites.

Silktide's channel hosts short, informative videos to support writing good content.

Courses

There are a multitude of courses out there. Many of them cost. Here are the most accessible, least expensive ones I have found.

  • Introduction to Content Design at Future Learn
  • Copywriting for Beginners at SkillShare.
  • Content Marketing Training at HubSpot Academy
  • Responsive Web Design at freeCodeCamp – if you’re interested in the bricks and mortar of web design
  • Build a Website with HTML, CSS and GitHub pages at Code Academy for more coding

Most of the above are free. You’re welcome!
